Hue (Thua Thien-Hue Province)

Places to Visit

  • Imperial City (Citadel)

    The former political, cultural, and religious center of the Nguyen Dynasty, the Imperial City of Hue is a UNESCO World Heritage site surrounded by thick walls and a moat. The citadel houses palaces, temples, and the Forbidden Purple City, which was once reserved exclusively for the emperor. Visitors can explore the well-preserved structures, learn about the Nguyen emperors, and enjoy the peaceful gardens within the complex.

  • Thien Mu Pagoda

    Overlooking the Perfume River, Thien Mu Pagoda is one of the oldest and most significant religious sites in Vietnam. The seven-story pagoda has become a symbol of Hue, and its history is closely tied to the political and religious movements in the region. The pagoda is also home to a car that transported a monk to Saigon in 1963, where he self-immolated in protest against the South Vietnamese government.

  • Tombs of the Nguyen Emperors

    Scattered along the banks of the Perfume River, the tombs of the Nguyen emperors are elaborate structures that reflect the personality and reign of each ruler. The tombs of Minh Mang, Tu Duc, and Khai Dinh are particularly renowned for their architectural beauty, blending traditional Vietnamese designs with influences from China and France.

Activities

  • Dragon Boat Cruise on the Perfume River

    A relaxing way to explore Hue is by taking a dragon boat cruise along the Perfume River, with stops at the Thien Mu Pagoda and the tombs of the emperors. The cruise offers scenic views of the city and its historical landmarks.

  • Cycling Around the Citadel

    Rent a bicycle and explore the historic Imperial City and its surrounding areas at your own pace. Cycling allows you to take in the beautiful landscapes and visit less-touristy spots around Hue.

Foods

  • Bun Bo Hue

    A spicy beef noodle soup that is a must-try in Hue. The dish is richer and more flavorful than the more famous Pho, with a broth that’s both spicy and fragrant, often made with lemongrass, chili, and shrimp paste. The dish typically includes slices of beef, pork knuckles, and congealed pig's blood.

  • Com Hen

    A unique dish made from baby clams, Com Hen is served with rice and a variety of herbs, nuts, and crispy rice crackers. It’s a specialty of Hue that reflects the city’s riverside location and is known for its complex and refreshing flavors

  • Banh Khoai

    A crispy pancake filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts, served with a side of herbs and a special dipping sauce. Similar to Banh Xeo but smaller and more flavorful, Banh Khoai is a popular street food in Hue.

Weather

Hue has a tropical monsoon climate with distinct wet and dry seasons. The best time to visit is from February to April, when the weather is dry and temperatures are mild.

Transportation

Hue is well-connected by train, bus, and plane. The city is small enough to explore by bicycle or on foot, and taxis and motorbikes are also readily available.

Hoi An (Quang Nam Province)

Places to Visit

  • Hoi An Ancient Town

    A UNESCO World Heritage site, Hoi An is famous for its well-preserved ancient streets, houses, and temples. This town was a major trading port from the 15th to the 19th century, attracting merchants from China, Japan, and Europe. The architecture of Hoi An reflects this multicultural past, with the Japanese Covered Bridge and Chinese Assembly Halls standing as iconic symbols of the town’s rich history.

  • My Son Sanctuary

    Located about 40 kilometers from Hoi An, My Son is a complex of Hindu temples built by the Champa Kingdom between the 4th and 14th centuries. These temples, dedicated to the god Shiva, showcase the spiritual and cultural history of the Cham people. My Son was the religious center of the Champa Kingdom, and its temples are among the most significant archaeological sites in Vietnam.

  • An Bang Beach

    Just a few kilometers from Hoi An’s ancient town, An Bang Beach is a beautiful stretch of sand that’s perfect for relaxing. The beach has become a popular spot for both locals and tourists, offering a tranquil escape with clear waters and stunning sunsets.

Activities

  • Take a Lantern-Making Workshop

    Hoi An is famous for its colorful lanterns. Join a workshop to learn how to make your own lantern, a perfect souvenir to take home. The experience is both creative and cultural, offering insights into Hoi An’s traditional crafts.

  • Explore the Night Market

    Visit the Hoi An Night Market, where you can find a variety of local foods, souvenirs, and handmade goods. The market is particularly vibrant during the monthly lantern festival, when the streets are lit up with hundreds of lanterns.

Foods

  • Cao Lau

    A signature dish of Hoi An, Cao Lau is a noodle dish made with thick rice noodles, pork, and fresh herbs, served with a small amount of broth. The noodles are unique to Hoi An, made using water from a specific local well, giving them a distinct texture.

  • Banh Mi Hoi An

    Often touted as one of the best banh mi in Vietnam, Hoi An’s version of the famous sandwich is packed with pork, pate, fresh vegetables, and a variety of sauces. Banh Mi Phuong is a popular spot, offering a delicious take on this classic Vietnamese sandwich.

  • White Rose Dumplings (Banh Bao Banh Vac)

    These delicate dumplings are made from rice paper filled with shrimp or pork, then steamed and topped with crispy shallots. They’re a specialty of Hoi An that you won’t find anywhere else, known for their light and delicate flavor.

Weather

The best time to visit Hoi An is from February to April and August to October when the weather is dry and temperatures are mild. Avoid the rainy season from October to January, as the town is prone to flooding.

Transportation

Hoi An is a pedestrian-friendly town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance. Bicycles and motorbikes are also popular ways to get around, and taxis are readily available.

Da Nang (Quang Nam Province)

Places to Visit

  • Marble Mountains

    A cluster of five marble and limestone hills, each named after the five elements: metal, wood, water, fire, and earth. The Marble Mountains are known for their caves, tunnels, and temples, which have been spiritual sanctuaries for centuries. Visitors can climb the stone steps to explore the caves, which house Buddhist shrines and statues, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding area from the mountain tops.

  • Ba Na Hills

    A hill station and resort area located about 30 kilometers west of Da Nang, Ba Na Hills offers a cool climate and stunning views. The area is famous for the Golden Bridge, a pedestrian bridge held up by giant stone hands, and the French Village, a recreation of a quaint European town. Originally established as a retreat by the French in the early 20th century, Ba Na Hills combines historical colonial architecture with modern entertainment, making it a unique destination in Central Vietnam.

  • Dragon Brigde

    Shaped like a dragon, this modern bridge is one of Da Nang’s most famous landmarks. The dragon, a symbol of power, nobility, and good fortune in Vietnamese culture, breathes fire and water during weekend evenings, making it a popular attraction for both locals and tourists. The bridge connects Da Nang’s airport with its major streets and is a prime spot for evening strolls and photography.

Activities

  • Relax on My Khe Beach

    One of the most beautiful beaches in Vietnam, My Khe offers white sand and clear water, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or surfing. The beach is well-maintained and lined with cafes and seafood restaurants, making it a popular spot for both locals and visitors.

  • Visit the Han Market

    Explore this bustling market for a taste of local life. It’s a great place to buy souvenirs, fresh produce, and sample local street food. The market is especially vibrant in the mornings, when vendors sell everything from fresh seafood to handicrafts.

Foods

  • Mi Quang

    A noodle dish originating from the region, Mi Quang is made with wide rice noodles, pork, shrimp, quail eggs, and fresh herbs, served with a small amount of broth. It’s a flavorful and colorful dish unique to central Vietnam, often garnished with peanuts and fried shallots for extra crunch.

  • Banh Xeo

    This savory pancake is made from rice flour, turmeric, and coconut milk, filled with pork, shrimp, and bean sprouts. It’s served with a variety of fresh herbs and a dipping sauce made from fish sauce, lime juice, garlic, and chili. Banh Xeo is a popular dish in Da Nang, enjoyed as both a street food and a restaurant favorite.

  • Banh Trang Cuon Thit Heo

    Thin slices of boiled pork belly served with rice paper, fresh herbs, and dipping sauce, allowing you to create your own fresh spring rolls. This dish is light, refreshing, and perfect for a quick meal or snack, showcasing the balance of flavors and textures in Vietnamese cuisine.

Weather

The best time to visit Da Nang is from February to May, when the weather is warm and dry. Transportation

Da Nang is well-connected by air, train, and bus. The city is easy to navigate by taxi, motorbike, or bicycle, and many of the attractions are within a short distance of each other.
