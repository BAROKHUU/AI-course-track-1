Southern Vietnam - Travel Guide

Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon)

Places to Visit

  • Reunification Palace

    Also known as the Independence Palace, this historic building was the site of the official end of the Vietnam War on April 30, 1975, when a North Vietnamese tank crashed through its gates. The palace, which was once the home and workplace of the President of South Vietnam, remains a symbol of the country's reunification and is preserved almost exactly as it was in the 1970s.

  • War Remnants Museum

    This museum provides a sobering look at the Vietnam War from the perspective of the Vietnamese. It features exhibits on the war's impact on the country, including graphic photographs, military equipment, and personal accounts. The museum serves as a powerful reminder of the conflict and its lasting effects on Vietnam.

  • Ben Thanh Market

    One of Ho Chi Minh City's most famous landmarks, Ben Thanh Market is a bustling marketplace where you can find everything from fresh produce to souvenirs. The market has been in operation since the French colonial period and remains a vital part of local life, offering visitors a taste of Saigon’s vibrant culture and cuisine.

Activities

  • Explore Cu Chi Tunnels

    Located about 70 kilometers from Ho Chi Minh City, the Cu Chi Tunnels are an extensive network of underground tunnels used by the Viet Cong during the Vietnam War. Visitors can crawl through the tunnels and learn about the ingenious tactics used by the Viet Cong during the conflict.

  • Take a Saigon River Cruise

    Enjoy a scenic cruise along the Saigon River, offering stunning views of the city skyline, especially at sunset. Dinner cruises are also available, combining local cuisine with the river’s tranquil beauty.

Foods

  • Pho Saigon

    Unlike the northern version, Pho Saigon is known for its sweeter broth, thanks to the addition of herbs like basil and cilantro. Pho Hoa Pasteur is a popular spot to try this southern variant of the dish, where the broth is clear and fragrant, and the meat is tender.

  • Banh Xeo

    A crispy pancake filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts, Banh Xeo is a southern specialty that is typically served with a side of lettuce and herbs, which are used to wrap the pancake before dipping it in sauce. The pancake is light and crispy, offering a delicious balance of flavors and textures.

  • Hu Tieu

    A popular noodle soup in southern Vietnam, Hu Tieu is typically made with a clear and slightly sweet broth, topped with pork, shrimp, quail eggs, and fresh herbs. It can be enjoyed as a soup or dry, with the broth served on the side. The dish is light yet flavorful, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Weather

Ho Chi Minh City has a tropical climate with two main seasons: the dry season (December to April) and the rainy season (May to November). The best time to visit is during the dry season, when the weather is warm and less humid.

Transportation

Taxis and motorbikes are the most common means of transportation in Ho Chi Minh City. Grab is widely available, and the city is also expanding its metro system. Walking is a great way to explore the central districts, especially around Ben Thanh Market and the Reunification Palace.

Mekong Delta

Places to Visit

  • Cai Rang Floating Market

    Located in Can Tho, Cai Rang is one of the largest floating markets in the Mekong Delta. Traders sell fresh produce directly from their boats, creating a vibrant scene on the water. The market operates early in the morning and offers a unique insight into the daily life of the local people.

  • Vinh Trang Pagoda

    Situated in My Tho, this beautiful Buddhist temple is famous for its mix of Vietnamese, Chinese, and Khmer architecture. The pagoda’s serene gardens and towering Buddha statues make it a peaceful stop on any Mekong Delta tour. Built in the mid-19th century, the pagoda reflects the religious diversity and cultural richness of the region.

  • Tra Su Cajuput Forest

    This protected forest area near Chau Doc is a haven for wildlife and a great place for eco-tours. Visitors can take a boat ride through the flooded forest, home to a variety of bird species, including storks, herons, and cormorants. The forest is not only a natural treasure but also a key part of the region's efforts to preserve its unique ecosystem.

Activities

  • Boat tour of the Delta

    Take a guided boat tour through the intricate network of rivers and canals that make up the Mekong Delta. These tours often include visits to local villages, fruit orchards, and traditional workshops, providing a glimpse into the rural life of the delta.

  • Cycling through villages

    Rent a bicycle and explore the delta's rural areas, where you can visit local markets, temples, and schools, and interact with the friendly locals. The flat terrain and scenic countryside make cycling an ideal way to experience the delta.

Foods

  • Banh Xeo Mekong

    The Mekong Delta version of Banh Xeo is larger and more flavorful, with a generous filling of shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts, wrapped in rice paper with fresh herbs. The pancake is often served with a sweet and tangy dipping sauce, making it a popular dish in the region.

  • Ca Kho To

    A traditional dish of caramelized fish in a clay pot, Ca Kho To is typically made with catfish or snakehead fish, slow-cooked with fish sauce, sugar, and spices to create a rich and savory flavor. The dish is often served with steamed rice and fresh vegetables, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.

  • Nem Nuong Cai Rang

    A specialty of the Cai Rang area, this dish consists of grilled pork skewers served with fresh vegetables, rice paper, and a dipping sauce. It’s a favorite among locals and visitors alike, known for its smoky flavor and juicy texture.

Weather

The Mekong Delta has a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. The best time to visit is during the dry season (December to April), but the region’s lush landscapes are also beautiful during the rainy season, when the rivers are full and the fields are green.

Transportation

The Mekong Delta is best explored by boat, but bicycles and motorbikes are also popular ways to get around. Local buses and private cars are available for longer distances between towns and cities in the delta.

Phu Quoc Island (Kien Giang Province)

Places to Visit

  • Sao Beach

    One of the most beautiful beaches on Phu Quoc Island, Sao Beach is known for its soft white sand and crystal-clear waters. It’s a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the natural beauty of the island. The beach's pristine condition and calm waters make it a favorite among both tourists and locals.

  • Phu Quoc Prison

    Also known as Coconut Tree Prison, this historical site was used during the French colonial period and the Vietnam War to imprison political dissidents and prisoners of war. Today, it serves as a museum, offering a glimpse into the harsh conditions endured by prisoners and the history of Vietnam’s struggle for independence.

  • Vinpearl Safari

    A unique attraction on Phu Quoc Island, Vinpearl Safari is Vietnam’s largest wildlife conservation park. It’s home to over 3,000 animals representing 150 species, including endangered species from around the world. The safari park is part of the island’s efforts to promote eco-tourism and wildlife conservation.

Activities

  • Snorkeling and Diving

    The waters around Phu Quoc are teeming with marine life, making it a popular destination for snorkeling and diving. The An Thoi Archipelago is particularly famous for its vibrant coral reefs, where you can see colorful fish, sea turtles, and other marine creatures.

  • Explore Local Pepper Farms

    Phu Quoc is known for its high-quality pepper, and visiting a pepper farm provides insight into the island’s agricultural traditions. You can see the pepper plants up close and learn about the harvesting process, and even buy some freshly harvested pepper to take home.

Foods

  • Goi Ca Trich

    A local specialty, this raw herring salad is made with fresh herring fillets, mixed with shredded coconut, onions, and herbs, and served with rice paper and dipping sauce. The dish is light, refreshing, and full of flavor, making it a must-try for seafood lovers.

  • Bun Quay

    This noodle soup is a Phu Quoc specialty, where diners can customize their bowls with ingredients like shrimp, pork, and fish paste. The noodles are freshly made and served with a flavorful broth, often accompanied by fresh herbs and lime.

  • Phu Quoc fish sauce

    Phu Quoc is famous for its fish sauce, known for its rich flavor and quality. Visiting a fish sauce factory is a popular activity, where you can learn about the production process and taste this essential Vietnamese condiment. Many visitors buy bottles of fish sauce as a souvenir of their trip to Phu Quoc.

Weather

Phu Quoc has a tropical climate with a wet season (May to October) and a dry season (November to April). The best time to visit is during the dry season, when the weather is ideal for beach activities and outdoor exploration.

Transportation

The island is best explored by motorbike or taxi. There are also shuttle buses available for some of the major tourist attractions, making it easy to get around the island.
