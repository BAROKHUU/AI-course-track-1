Northern Vietnam - Travel Guide

Hanoi (Capital City)

Places to Visit

  • Hoan Kiem Lake and Ngoc Son Temple

    A serene lake in the heart of Hanoi, Hoan Kiem is steeped in the legend of Emperor Le Loi returning a magical sword to the Golden Turtle God. Ngoc Son Temple, on a small island in the lake, honors General Tran Hung Dao, who defeated the Mongols in the 13th century. Cross the iconic red Huc Bridge to reach this peaceful temple, a place of reflection and history.

  • Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum

    This solemn site houses the embalmed body of Ho Chi Minh, the leader who declared Vietnam’s independence. Located in Ba Dinh Square, the mausoleum is a symbol of national pride. Visitors can also explore the Presidential Palace and Ho Chi Minh's stilt house in the surrounding complex.

  • Thang Long Imperial Citadel

    A UNESCO World Heritage site, this citadel was the political heart of Vietnam for over a thousand years. Visitors can explore ancient gates, palaces, and archaeological remains, uncovering layers of history from the Ly, Tran, and Le dynasties.

Activities

  • Cyclo Ride in the Old Quarter

    Experience the bustling streets of Hanoi's Old Quarter, where you can see traditional trades and vibrant street life.

  • Water Puppet Show

    A unique Vietnamese art form that originated in the Red River Delta, telling stories through puppets dancing on water.

Foods

  • Pho

    The quintessential Vietnamese dish, a fragrant noodle soup usually served with beef or chicken. The broth is simmered for hours with star anise, cinnamon, and cloves, creating a deep, rich flavor. Pho Gia Truyen Bat Dan is a famous spot to try this delicacy.

  • Bun Cha

    Grilled pork served with rice noodles, fresh herbs, and dipping sauce. This Hanoi specialty is often enjoyed with a side of fried spring rolls. Bun Cha Huong Lien, known for hosting former US President Obama, is a must-visit for an authentic experience.

  • Cha Ca La Vong

    Grilled fish marinated with turmeric and dill, served with vermicelli noodles, peanuts, and herbs. This dish is cooked tableside, adding to the dining experience. Cha Ca La Vong is the most famous restaurant serving this dish, which has become synonymous with Hanoi cuisine.

Weather

Hanoi experiences four distinct seasons. The best times to visit are Spring (March-April) and Autumn (September-November), when the weather is mild and comfortable.

Transportation

Taxis and Grab are widely available. The Old Quarter is best explored on foot or by cyclo, allowing you to soak in the historic charm of the area.

Ha Long Bay (Quang Ninh Province)

Places to Visit

  • Halong Bay

    Renowned for its emerald waters and limestone islands, Halong Bay is a natural wonder steeped in legend. According to myth, dragons descended to protect Vietnam, creating the bay's unique landscape. Cruises offer opportunities to explore floating villages, caves, and the bay’s stunning karsts.

  • Sung Sot Cave

    Known as the "Cave of Surprises," this large cave on Bo Hon Island features impressive stalactite and stalagmite formations. The cave has two chambers, offering spectacular views of the bay from its entrance.

Activities

  • Cruise the Bay

    Explore the stunning landscapes, floating villages, and hidden coves on a day or overnight cruise. Cruises often include activities such as kayaking, visiting pearl farms, and enjoying a seafood lunch on board.

  • Kayaking

    Paddle through the calm waters, getting up close to the limestone karsts. This activity allows you to explore less accessible areas, such as hidden lagoons and caves.

  • Swimming and Sunbathing

    Relax on the pristine beaches scattered throughout the bay. Titop Island is a popular spot for swimming, offering clear waters and a sandy beach.

Foods

  • Seafood

    Ha Long Bay is renowned for its fresh seafood, including squid, crab, and shrimp. Seafood is often grilled or steamed and served with local herbs and dipping sauces. Many cruises offer the option to catch and cook your own seafood, providing a hands-on culinary experience.

  • Cha Muc

    A specialty made from freshly caught squid, ground into a paste, and deep-fried. This dish is often served with sticky rice or as a topping for noodles.

  • Ngan

    A unique shellfish found in the waters of Ha Long Bay, often grilled, steamed, or cooked in soup. Ngán has a distinct briny flavor and is typically served with a spicy dipping sauce.

Weather

The best time to visit Ha Long Bay is from October to April, with cooler weather and clear skies. Avoid the summer months (June to August) due to the potential for storms.

Transportation

Buses and private cars are available from Hanoi to Ha Long Bay, taking about 3.5 to 4 hours. Once in Ha Long, boats and cruises are the primary modes of transportation.

Sapa (Lao Cai Province)

Places to Visit

  • Fansipan Mountain

    The highest peak in Vietnam, Fansipan is accessible by a challenging trek or via cable car. The summit offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and terraced rice fields, earning it the nickname "Roof of Indochina."

  • A traditional Hmong village near Sapa town, Cat Cat offers a glimpse into local life with its preserved customs and crafts. The village is also home to a picturesque waterfall, where you can relax and enjoy the natural surroundings.

  • Muong Hoa Valley

    Famous for its terraced rice fields and ancient rock carvings, Muong Hoa Valley is a must-visit for hikers. The valley’s stunning scenery and cultural heritage make it a highlight of any trip to Sapa.

Activities

  • Trekking

    Sapa is renowned for its trekking opportunities, with trails leading to various ethnic minority villages. Trekking routes range from easy day hikes to multi-day adventures, offering breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Visit Local Markets

    Explore vibrant markets where ethnic minority groups sell handmade goods, textiles, and jewelry. The Bac Ha Market, held every Sunday, is particularly famous for its colorful atmosphere and unique products.

Foods

  • Thang Co

    A traditional Hmong soup made from horse meat, bones, and organs, flavored with various herbs and spices. This dish is often served during festivals and community gatherings, offering a taste of Hmong culinary traditions.

  • Cap Nach Pork

    A specialty of the region, this dish is made from a small breed of pig raised by the ethnic minority groups. The pork is marinated with herbs and spices, then grilled or fried, known for its tender and flavorful meat.

  • Salmon Hotpot

    A local specialty made from fresh salmon, served with vegetables and mushrooms. The cool climate of Sapa is surprisingly suitable for farming salmon, making this hotpot dish a unique treat.

Weather

Sapa is known for its cool climate, with temperatures dropping significantly at night. The best time to visit is from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and the rice terraces are at their most beautiful. Winter (December to February) can be cold and foggy, with occasional snowfall in the higher elevations.

Transportation

Sapa is accessible by overnight train or bus from Hanoi. Once in Sapa, motorbikes, taxis, and guided tours are available for exploring the area. The town itself is walkable, but for visiting surrounding villages and attractions, renting a motorbike or hiring a local guide is recommended.
