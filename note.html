Final Notes for Your Journey Through Vietnam

As you conclude your journey through the captivating regions of Vietnam, here are some final tips and important information to ensure your trip is both safe and respectful:

Emergency Numbers

  • 113: Police - For any police-related emergencies such as theft, assault, or any situation where you need law enforcement assistance.
  • 114: Fire Department - For reporting fires or requesting fire brigade services.
  • 115: Ambulance and Medical Emergencies - If you or someone in your group requires immediate medical attention.

Etiquette Tips

  • Greetings: A simple nod or a slight bow is considered polite. Shaking hands is common, but avoid strong or aggressive handshakes.
  • Use of Hands: When giving or receiving something, use both hands or your right hand with the left hand supporting the right elbow as a sign of respect.
  • Respect for Elders: Always show respect to older people by greeting them first and offering them the best seat.

Religious Customs and Dress Codes

  • Buddhist Temples: When visiting temples or pagodas, dress modestly. Shoulders and knees should be covered. Remove your shoes before entering the temple buildings.
  • Sacred Sites: Refrain from touching religious objects or statues unless permitted. Always ask for permission before taking photographs inside temples.
